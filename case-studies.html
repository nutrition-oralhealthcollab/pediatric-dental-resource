Case of 4yo with Sensory Feeding Disorder

medical_information

Patient: Leo M.
Age: 4.2 Years

Diagnosis: Avoidant/Restrictive Food Intake (ARFID)

Patient History

Patient presents with significant hypersensitivity to textures. Parents report a "beige diet" consisting primarily of processed carbohydrates. Leo exhibits gagging when introduced to fibrous textures or strong olfactory stimuli. Previous attempts at occupational therapy (OT) showed marginal improvement over 6 months.

Nutritional Assessment

Below 15th percentile for BMI-for-age. Laboratory workups indicate mild Vitamin D and Iron deficiency. Dental screening reveals early-stage demineralization on second primary molars due to lack of self-cleansing fibrous food intake.

Decision Analysis

What is the most appropriate next step in Leo's multidisciplinary management plan?

Clinical Insight

Recent data suggests that sensory feeding disorders often require a multimodal approach involving SLPs and Dietitians to address both the physical mechanics and nutritional gaps.
