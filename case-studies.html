Pierre Robin Sequence & G-Tube Dependence

face_6 child_care

Patient: Maya R.
Age: 3.1 Years

Diagnosis: PRS, Gastrostomy Dependent

History & Biofilm Management

Maya presents with micrognathia and status post cleft palate repair. Primarily G-tube dependent with minimal oral bolus, she exhibits rapid calculus formation and heavy biofilm accumulation. AAPD standards highlight that non-oral feeding leads to decreased salivary flow and reduced mechanical clearance, necessitating aggressive preventive management despite the lack of traditional cariogenic oral intake.

Interdisciplinary Surgical Coordination

As part of the AAP-recommended interdisciplinary team, the surgical plan includes Mandibular Distraction Osteogenesis (MDO). Dental clearance is critical to prevent perioperative infection; however, care must be balanced with Maya's sensory sensitivities related to PRS. Biofilm control is the primary objective to stabilize oral health before the planned distraction.

Physiology Note: Frontiers Research Insight

"In PRS patients with limited masticatory stimulation, salivary proteome analysis indicates a shift toward a pro-inflammatory state. Reduced salivary flow rate (hypoptyalism) directly correlates with accelerated calcium phosphate precipitation on existing biofilm, creating tenacious calculus that harbors pathogenic anaerobic flora, increasing risk for surgical site infection during mandibular distraction."

AAPD/ABPD Guidance Summary

In complex PRS cases, non-oral feeders require specialized biofilm management as calculus forms rapidly without mechanical cleansing. ABPD standards emphasize interdisciplinary planning where dental clearance precedes major craniofacial surgery to optimize surgical outcomes and minimize bacteremia risk.
