GI-Dental Clinical Protocols

Consolidated technical guidelines for pediatric patients with special health care needs (SHCN).

shield_with_heart

I. Erosion Prevention (GERD/Reflux)

Protocols for managing gastric acid exposure and enamel stability.

water_ph Salivary Neutralization

  • • Immediate water rinsing post-reflux episode.
  • • Sialagogue therapy (sugar-free xylitol) to boost bicarbonate.
  • • Wait 60 minutes before mechanical brushing.

auto_fix Remineralization

  • • 5000 ppm Fluoride toothpaste (Rx strength).
  • • CPP-ACP (Casein Phosphopeptide-Amorphous Calcium Phosphate).
  • • Professional fluoride varnish every 3 months.

II. Cariogenic Potential

Nutrition and supplemental exposure management.

warning

Thickened Liquids & Supplements

High-viscosity supplements increase carbohydrate retention time. Patients using thickened liquids require enhanced oral clearance protocols immediately after feeding cycles.

Risk Factor

Enteral nutrition "grazing" patterns

Mitigation

Scheduled bolus feeds over continuous sips

medical_services

III. Hygiene for G-Tube / Non-Oral Feeders

Managing the unique oral environment of enteral nutrition.

Biofilm Control

Twice daily soft brushing with 0.12% Chlorhexidine to manage altered microbial diversity.

Calculus Mgmt

Frequent (3mo) prophy due to higher salivary pH and calcium concentrations in G-tube users.

Sialagogues

Artificial saliva or stimulation to maintain ductal patency and oral mucosal integrity.

IV. Referral Triggers

Erosive Crisis

Dentin exposure or hypersensitivity preventing adequate oral intake.

Aspiration Risk

Heavy biofilm in medically fragile patients with dysphagia history.

Anesthesia Coordination

Need for GA restorative work requiring GI/Cardiac clearance.

family_history Care Access Support

Navigating coverage for SHCN patients is essential. Direct families to the national portal for Medicaid/CHIP details.
