Dental Implications of GERD

Evidence-based clinical guidelines for identifying and managing Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease in pediatric dental patients.

shield

Erosion Prevention Strategies

Immediate Response

Instruct patients NOT to brush teeth for at least 30-60 minutes following a reflux episode. Rinse with water or sodium bicarbonate solution immediately.

Salivary Stimulation

Recommend sugar-free xylitol gum to stimulate protective salivary flow and neutralize gastric acids through increased bicarbonate production.

list_alt Dietary Modifications

  • check_circle Avoid carbonated beverages and acidic citrus fruits in the evening.
  • check_circle Minimize snacking between meals to allow salivary pH recovery.

Screening for Non-Dental Providers

Oral Inspection

Palatal Erosion Patterns

Non-dental providers should check for "cupping" on molar cusps and thinning of enamel on the palatal surfaces of upper incisors.

Questionnaire

BEWE Scoring

The Basic Erosive Wear Examination (BEWE) tool allows quick categorization of risk: Score 0-18. Scores >13 require immediate specialist referral.

groups

Referral Protocols

Step 1: Clinical Suspicion

Presence of dental erosion (Tooth Wear Index >2) alongside symptoms like chronic coughing or waterbrash.

Step 2: GI Collaboration

Standardized referral to Pediatric Gastroenterology. Include photographic evidence of palatal thinning.

Step 3: Joint Management

Establish 3-month dental recall cycle to monitor erosion progression while GI manages pharmacological therapy.

URGENCY INDICATOR

Refer immediately if dentinal sensitivity prohibits oral hygiene maintenance.
