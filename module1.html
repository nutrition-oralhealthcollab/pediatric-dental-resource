Bridging the gap between dental anatomy and interdisciplinary feeding care for SLPs, Dietitians, and GI specialists.

Why this matters for feeding teams

As feeding team providers, you interact daily with children whose oral structures directly influence their ability to eat, drink, and maintain adequate nutrition. This module builds a shared vocabulary so your observations during feeding assessments can be communicated effectively to dental colleagues, and dental findings can be meaningfully integrated into your care plans.

Clinical Significance of Primary Dentition in Swallowing

restaurant

Masticatory Efficiency

The presence of molars dictates the transition from munching patterns to true rotary chewing.

speech_to_text

Bolus Containment

Anterior teeth provide the necessary seal to prevent premature bolus loss during the oral phase.

warning Clinical Red Flag

"Children with missing primary molars often compensate by over-utilizing the tongue for crushing, leading to increased fatigue during meals and a preference for pureed textures long after the developmental norm."
