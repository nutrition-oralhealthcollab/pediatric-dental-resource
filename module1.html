analytics Clinical Significance in Swallowing

The primary dentition is not merely a precursor to permanent teeth; it serves as a critical structural anchor for the oral phase of swallowing. Feeding specialists (SLPs/OTs) must recognize that tooth eruption patterns directly influence bolus management and lingual coordination.

expand Vertical Dimension

Shared Vocabulary

  • Primary Dentition Deciduous or "baby" teeth (20 total).
  • Centric Occlusion The relation of opposing surfaces of the teeth.
  • Bolus Containment Ability of teeth and lips to keep food in the oral cavity.
  • Exfoliation Natural loss of primary teeth.

Multidisciplinary Integration

Dietitians should consider tooth availability when prescribing texture gradients. For example, a child lacking primary molars (typically erupting between 13-33 months) may struggle with stage 3 solids despite age-appropriate motor patterns.

SLP Priority RD Consideration
medical_information

Gastrointestinal Connection

Chronic GERD can lead to dental erosion, significantly increasing tooth sensitivity and resulting in food refusal. This "pain-based refusal" often presents as behavioral but is physiologically driven by enamel loss.

Clinical Red Flag

Monitor for thinning enamel on the lingual surfaces of maxillary incisors in patients with diagnosed reflux.
