Early Childhood Caries (ECC)

CRITICAL DETECTION

Early detection by non-dental providers is the frontline defense. Identify "white spot" lesions along the gingival margin before cavitation occurs.

STAGE 1: INCIPENT

Chalky white opacities; demineralization of the enamel surface.

STAGE 2: CAVITATED

Brown/Yellow lesions with structural loss. Immediate referral required.

Oral Candidiasis

Characterized by "curd-like" white plaques that can be scraped off, revealing an erythematous base. Common in infants and immunocompromised patients.

Herpetic Lesions

Vesicular lesions that rupture to form painful ulcers. Primary Herpetic Gingivostomatitis often involves high fever and significant irritability.

Urgent Referral Pathways

When should you bypass routine scheduling? Clinical indicators for 24-48 hour specialist evaluation.

01

Facial Swelling

Asymmetry, cellulitis, or intraoral fluctuance. Airway risk.

02

Trauma

Avulsion of permanent teeth or deep lacerations with mobility.

03

Progressive Pain

Interruption of sleep or eating, unresponsive to standard analgesics.
