Module 03 // Clinical Intelligence

Diet, Nutrition, and
Oral Health.

Registered dietitians possess a uniquely powerful position to identify oral health risks. This module translates core dental nutrition science into actionable practice, identifying how early childhood caries act as a barrier to optimal caloric intake.

Core Insight

"Early childhood caries can be a hidden barrier to optimal caloric intake in sensory feeding patients."

Cariogenic Properties of Thickened Liquids

Impact of viscosity on oral clearance and enamel health

Critical Risk

Thickening agents often introduce hidden fermentable carbohydrates. The viscosity increases oral clearance time, keeping sugars in contact with enamel longer.

  • check_circle Starch-based thickeners
  • check_circle High-calorie supplements (ONS)
pH Clearance Comparison (Min)
Standard Water 2 min
Thickened Liquid (Lvl 3) 22 min
science

Enamel Mineralization

Vital micro-nutrients influence post-eruptive tooth resilience. Calcium, Phosphate, and Vitamin D levels must be optimized in the early dietetic plan.

Managing 'Grazing'

Sensory feeding patients often prefer frequent, small intakes. This constant "acid bath" prevents critical remineralization windows.

Dietetic Strategy
Scheduled "Structured Snacks" instead of ad-lib grazing.
Counter-measure
Immediate water rinse following high-viscosity intake.

Care Plan Integration

Clinical application for multidisciplinary teams

01 Assessment

Evaluate frequency of fermentable carb exposure alongside total caloric intake goals.

02 Intervention

Adjust supplemental thickening agents to non-cariogenic alternatives where clinically possible.

03 Collaboration

Coordinate with Pediatric Dentist for high-risk fluoride varnish schedules and sealant needs.
