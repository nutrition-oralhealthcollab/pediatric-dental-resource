Exploring the intersection of dietetic interventions and pediatric dental outcomes. A clinical guide for dietitians in multidisciplinary care.

warning

Cariogenic Properties of Supplements

Thickened Liquids

Commonly used for dysphagia, thickened liquids often contain starch-based agents that increase oral clearance time, prolonging exposure to fermentable carbohydrates.

Clinical Insight

Reduced salivary flow or physical barriers increase the risk of rapid enamel demineralization.

High-Calorie Supplements

Enteral formulas and oral supplements (shakes) frequently have high sugar content to meet caloric needs, presenting a significant dental challenge.

  • High viscosity = increased stickiness
  • Frequency is often more damaging than volume
schedule

Managing 'Grazing'

Continuous exposure to food prevents the pH of the mouth from returning to a neutral state, maintaining a constant acid attack on enamel.

Integrated Care Plans

Dietitians are uniquely positioned to integrate dental risk screening into standard nutritional assessments, particularly for children with complex needs.

checklist Dietetic Care Plan Checklist

check_circle

Review Supplement Timing

Advise intake with meals rather than sip-grazing between meals.

check_circle

Oral Rinse Advocacy

Encourage rinsing with water immediately after consuming thickened liquids.

check_circle

Identify 'Hidden' Sugars

Educate families on the fermentable carbohydrate content of thickening agents.

check_circle

Dental Referral Trigger

Standardize referrals for patients on high-carbohydrate nutritional support.
