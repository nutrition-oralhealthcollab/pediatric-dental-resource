Non-Nutritive Sucking (NNS)

Digit vs. Pacifier Use

Evaluation of intensity, duration, and frequency. Digit habits are often more recalcitrant and cause greater dental arch deformation.

Dental Consequences

Anterior open bite, increased overjet, and posterior crossbite due to buccal musculature pressure during prolonged NNS.

Myofunctional Impact

  • check_circle Low resting tongue posture
  • check_circle Mouth breathing habituation
  • check_circle Atypical swallowing patterns

Sensory Oral Aversion

Barriers to feeding success often manifest as defensive behaviors toward specific textures or temperatures.

Clinical Assessment Checklist

Based on the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry (AAPD) policy on oral habits.

Tongue Thrust Analysis

Observe during saliva swallow and speech tasks (s, z, t, d, n, l).

Lip Competence

Assess mentalis muscle strain during lip closure at rest.

Arch Morphology

Visual check for V-shaped maxillary arch or high vaulted palate.

Airway Patency

Collaborate with ENT if chronic mouth breathing or snoring present.

Habit History

Detailed parent interview on pacifier weaning or digit use intensity.

Feeding Readiness

Assessment of bolus management and gag reflex sensitivity.

Tongue Thrust & Orofacial Myofunctional Disorders (OMD)

Tongue thrust is more than a speech sound error; it's a physiological imbalance. When the tongue pushes forward against or between the teeth, it can exert up to 500 grams of pressure, causing significant dental displacement over time.

80% Co-morbidity with OMD
4.2x Relapse Risk
